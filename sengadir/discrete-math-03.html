3.7 Predicate Calculus

3.7.3 Quantifiers, Free and Bound Variables

We use ∀x to denote for all x and ∃x to denote there exists x. These symbols ∀ and ∃ are known as quantifiers.

Example 3.38

Consider the statement ∃x ∀y ⁡ P xy

  • There exists x such that for all y, P xy .
  • すべての y に対して P xy が成り立つ、そのような x が少なくともひとつは存在するか?
  • Suppose P xy is defined as x2 − 1 ⁢ y =0 where x,y ∈ℝ . There exists x (1 and −1) such that for all y, P xy holds true.
  • Suppose P xy is defined as 2⁢x − y =0 where x,y ∈ℝ . There is no real number x such that for all y, P xy holds true.
Consider the statement ∀x ∃y ⁡ P xy
  • For all x there exists y such that P xy .
  • 個々の x について、それに対応した y をうまく選んで P xy を真にすることができるか?
  • Suppose P xy is defined as x2 − 1 ⁢ y =0 where x,y ∈ℝ . The statement is true for all x by taking y=0 .
  • Suppose P xy is defined as 2⁢x − y =0 where x,y ∈ℝ . The statement is true for all x by taking y= 2⁢x .
P xy is a statement function of two variables. Given that x∈A and y∈B, its domain must be a cartesian product ( A×B ) . Let D₁ = 12345 × 12345 and D₂ = ℕ×ℕ . We take The statement ∃x ∀y ⁡ P xy is
  • true with domain D₁ because 5≥y for all y ∈ 12345 , but
  • false with domain D₂ because we can not find an x∈ℕ such that x≥y for all y∈ℕ.
The statement ∀x ∃y ⁡ P xy is
  • true with domain D₁ because x=5 can be chosen for each y, and
  • true with domain D₂ because for each y, we can take x=y.
