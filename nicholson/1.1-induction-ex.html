Exercise 1.1 – 9

A straight line segment joining two distinct points on a circle is called a secant. For n≥1 , draw n secants with no two identical. Show that the resulting regions can be unambiguously colored black and white (where unambiguously means that no two regions sharing a straight line boundary are of the same color).

Solution. It is clear if n=1 . Given k secants, we assume all regions are colored unambiguously. Now add one secant. On one side of this secant, leave all regions the orignial color (including the new regions of that side created by the new secant). On the other side, interchange colors everywhere (including those regions newly created). This is an unambiguous coloring. ▮
